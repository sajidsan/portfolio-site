VISUALIZING COMPLEXITY

The ID
Alumni Network

When I first looking into grad programs for design, running into IIT’s Institute of Design was a happy accident. I was enticed by the programs’ focus on the broad application of design, I kept on running into conflicting messages when doing research on the program. I started poking around to find out more but, even by the time I had decided that it was the right fit, the school still felt pretty mysterious. After getting to the Institute of Design, I realized others felt the same way and I started this project.

It wasn’t until I started the program that I realized so much of the program’s identity crisis was due to the fact that it had changed so much over time.

While the program was founded in the pragmatism and minimalistic craft of the bauhaus, its most recent leadership had driven into the direction of structured planning and innovation.

When I started to break up graduates by degree type, I first noticed that different that graduates from different eras had started clustering around industries in different areas of the country.

The success of the project was its ability to acting as a conversation piece for Alumni that had never had a good enough excuse to connect with each other.
